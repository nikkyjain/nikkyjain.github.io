The substances (dravya) are of two kinds, the soul (jīva) and the non-soul (ajīva). Further, the soul (jīva) is of the nature of consciousness (chetanā) that manifests in form of cognition (upayoga). Starting from the physical matter (pudgala), the other substances (dravya) are inanimate (acetana); these comprise the non-soul (ajīva) substances (dravya).
