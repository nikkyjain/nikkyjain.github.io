From the empirical or phenomenal point of view (vyavahara naya), that which is living at present, will continue to live in the future, and was living in the past, through its four principles of organism (prānas – strength bala prāna; senses or indriya prāna; duration of age or āyuh prāna; and respiration or ucchvāsa-nihshvāsa prāna), is the jīva. From the transcendental or noumenal point of view (nishcaya naya), that which has consciousness is the jīva.
